## OCSD-Flock MOU (Official Contract)

**Source:** [CA-Orange County MOU Fully Executed](https://cdn.muckrock.com/foia_files/2026/01/15/CA-Orange_county_MOU_Fully_executed.pdf)

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Parties** | Flock Group, Inc. & Orange County Sheriff's Department |
| **OCSD Signer** | Sheriff Donald D. Barnes |
| **Flock Signer** | Mark Smith, General Counsel |
| **Date Signed** | June 1, 2022 (OCSD) / June 3, 2022 (Flock) |
| **Term** | 5 years |
| **Default Data Retention** | 30 days |
| **Financial Commitment** | None required by OCSD |
| **DocuSign ID** | 8EF4516D-B9CF-47BA-9F54-53AADED29675 |

**Key Contract Terms:**
- OCSD gets "non-exclusive, non-transferable right to access" Flock services
- Videos stored for no longer than 30 days
- Agency must use service "for purposes authorized by law"
- **No specific prohibition on federal agency sharing**
- Flock retains ownership of all data and recordings
- Either party can terminate with 30-90 days notice

### Vigilant Solutions and ICE Access (CORRECTED)

**IMPORTANT CORRECTION:** ICE does NOT have a direct contract with Vigilant Solutions. The relationship is:

```
Vigilant Solutions (collects ALPR data)
       ↓
West Publishing / Thomson Reuters (packages data in CLEAR platform)
       ↓
ICE (purchases access via Thomson Reuters contract)
```

## Flock Safety and ICE/Federal Access

### Official Position vs. Reality

**Flock's Official Claim:** No contract with ICE or DHS sub-agencies. Data not shared without local agency approval.

**Reality:** ICE and federal agencies access Flock data through multiple methods:

### How Federal Agencies Access Flock Data

#### 1. "Side Door" Access
Local police run searches **on behalf of ICE/CBP**. Over 4,000 immigration-related lookups conducted this way nationwide.
- Warren County, VA Sheriff confirmed searches because "federal immigration authorities asked"
- Metro Atlanta: 47+ ICE-related searches (Jan-Mar 2025)
- Source: [VPM Investigation](https://www.vpm.org/news/2025-07-07/vcij-immigration-enforcement-flock-safety-license-plate-readers-poggenklass)

#### 2. "Back Door" Access
Configuration issues enabled sharing without explicit authorization.
- At least 10 Washington state agencies had CBP access enabled without knowing
- Source: [Immigration Policy Tracking](https://immpolicytracking.org/policies/reported-ice-accessing-flock-automated-license-plate-reader-cameras-via-local-law-enforcement/)

#### 3. Pilot Programs (Now "Paused")
Flock CEO admitted running "limited pilots" with CBP and HSI giving direct access.
- 404 Media reported CBP had direct system access
- Flock claims pilots are now paused
- Source: [Flock Statement](https://www.flocksafety.com/blog/statement-in-response-to-recent-reports)

#### 4. National/State Lookup Network
Federal agencies could query through connected agencies (Flock claims this is now disabled).

### Scale of Immigration Searches

| Location | Immigration Searches | Time Period |
|----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Virginia | ~3,000 | 12 months |
| Denver | 1,400+ | Jun 2024 - Apr 2025 |
| SFPD | 1.6M illegal out-of-state searches (19+ for ICE) | 2024-2025 |

### Orange County Specifically

**OCSD and multiple OC agencies were found sharing ALPR data with ICE and CBP in violation of California law (SB 34).**

- OCSD conducted searches on behalf of CBP/Border Patrol
- Source: [CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/economy/technology/2025/06/california-police-sharing-license-plate-reader-data/)

### Cities That Canceled Flock Contracts Over ICE Concerns

- Chicago, IL
- Richmond, VA
- Cities in Massachusetts, North Carolina, Illinois
- Charlottesville, VA (cut off federal access)

### Contract Language to Watch

Huntington Beach contract includes clause allowing Flock to share data with authorities if "legally required" - a potential loophole.

### Cities with "Prohibited Use" Policies (May Not Be Enforced)

These OC cities officially prohibit immigration enforcement use:
- Buena Park
- Costa Mesa

However, "side door" access through other agencies can bypass these policies.

## Key Legal Context

### California SB 34
- Requires law enforcement to adopt detailed ALPR usage and privacy policies
- Restricts access to authorized purposes
- Requires regular audits to prevent misuse
- **Prohibits sharing ALPR data with federal agencies**

### 2025 Data Sharing Violations

Despite SB 34, multiple California agencies violated the law:
- OCSD shared data with ICE/CBP
- SFPD allowed 1.6M+ illegal out-of-state searches
- Santa Cruz, Capitola, Watsonville data accessed by federal agencies
