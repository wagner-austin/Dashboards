File a Public Records Request

CPRA = California Public Records Act. It's your legal right to request government documents. It's completely legal and commonly used by journalists, researchers, and citizens. The government cannot retaliate against you for filing a request.

Template: Irvine PD ALPR Data Sharing

For Police/ALPR records: Public Safety Records Form

Email: irvinequickrec@cityofirvine.org | Phone: 949-724-6281

General portal: cityofirvine.org/office-records-and-information

Subject: California Public Records Act Request - ALPR Data Sharing

Dear City Clerk:

Pursuant to the California Public Records Act (Government Code Section 6250 et seq.), I request copies of the following records related to the Irvine Police Department's Automated License Plate Reader (ALPR) program:

1. CURRENT VENDOR CONTRACT
   - The complete contract/agreement between the City of Irvine (or Irvine PD) and any ALPR vendor (Vigilant Solutions/Motorola Solutions, Flock Safety, or other)
   - All amendments or modifications to said contract
   - Current contract term and renewal dates

2. NVLS PARTICIPATION STATUS
   - Documentation showing whether Irvine PD ALPR data is currently uploaded to Vigilant Solutions' National Vehicle Location Service (NVLS)
   - Any opt-in or opt-out documentation for national/regional data sharing networks

3. AGENCY ACCESS LIST
   - A complete list of all external agencies that currently have access to query Irvine PD's ALPR database
   - Any Memoranda of Understanding (MOUs) or data sharing agreements with other agencies

4. FEDERAL AGENCY ACCESS
   - Any records of requests from federal agencies (including but not limited to ICE, CBP, HSI, DHS) to access Irvine PD ALPR data
   - Any records of Irvine PD personnel conducting ALPR searches on behalf of federal agencies

5. FLOCK NETWORK AUDIT LOGS
   - Export from Flock Safety's "Network Audit" feature showing all external agencies that have searched Irvine PD's ALPR database between January 1, 2025 and present
   - Export from Flock Safety showing "Networks that I'm sharing" configuration
   - Any audit logs showing searches run by IPD through other agencies' Flock cameras

If any records are available electronically, I request they be provided electronically to reduce copying costs.

Per Government Code Section 6253(c), I request a response within 10 days.

Thank you,
[Your Name]

Legal Context

California SB 34

Requires ALPR usage policies, restricts access to authorized purposes, requires audits, and prohibits sharing with federal agencies.

California Values Act (SB 54)

Prohibits local law enforcement from sharing personal information for immigration enforcement.

2025 Violations Found

CalMatters and SF Standard reported multiple CA agencies violated SB 34 by sharing ALPR data with ICE/CBP.
